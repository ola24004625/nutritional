Types of sleeping difficulty

Sleeping difficulties refer to problems that affect a person's ability to get restful and uninterrupted sleep. This can encompass a variety of sleep-related issues.

  • Insominia
  • Narcolespy
  • Parasomnias

Improving Sleep Hygiene

It's important to identify whether your sleep difficulties are due to temporary factors (like stress) or if they might be related to a medical issue that requires professional treatment. If sleep problems persist, consulting with a healthcare provider or sleep specialist is a good idea to explore further solutions.

What are the effects of better sleeping habits

Reduced Stress and Anxiety: Adequate sleep helps regulate the body's stress hormones. With better sleep habits, you're likely to experience less anxiety, feel more emotionally stable, and handle stress more effectively. Chronic sleep deprivation is linked to an increased risk of depression. By improving sleep habits, you support your mental health and reduce the likelihood of experiencing depressive symptoms.

Good sleep is essential for a well-functioning immune system. Regularly getting adequate rest helps the body defend itself against infections and illnesses. Sleep helps regulate hormones related to hunger (like ghrelin and leptin), helping to control appetite and potentially reduce the risk of overeating or weight gain.
